Apple Pork Chops

  • salt and pepper
  • 2 pounds bone in pork chops
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 medium apples
  • half small yellow onion
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • quarter teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • pinch of nutmeg
  • 1 and a half teaspoons flour
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  1. Season both sides of the pork chops with salt and pepper.
  2. Set a skillet over medium heat. Add the oil and heat. Add the pork chops and cook for 4 minutes on each side.
  3. Remove the pork chops and set aside. Add the onions and apples to the pan. Season with rosemary, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Cook until tender, about 5 to 7 minutes.
  4. Whisk in the flour. Then add the chicken stock to the pan. Add the pork chops back to the skillet, being sure to add in all of the drippings that are on the plate.
  5. Continue cooking over medium heat until pork chops have reached an internal temperature of 145 degrees, about 2-3 minutes. Taste the sauce and add more salt and pepper to taste.
